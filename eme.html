Our Projects

Artisan Workshops and Training

Establish workshops where skilled artisans can pass down their craftsmanship to younger generations. This would include activities such as traditional boat building, weaving, pottery, and jewelry making.

Indigenous Language Preservation

Support initiatives to document and revitalize indigenous languages, ensuring that these vital aspects of Palawan's culture are not lost.

Cultural Policy Advocacy

Advocate for policies that protect and promote Palawan's cultural heritage, including funding for cultural preservation projects and the enforcement of laws against cultural appropriation.

Cultural Mentorship Programs

Pair experienced cultural practitioners with aspiring artists and performers to provide mentorship and training, helping to pass down skills and knowledge.

Cultural Education Programs

Develop educational programs in schools and communities that teach Palawan's history, languages, and customs. Encourage the inclusion of cultural subjects in the curriculum to ensure that the younger generation values and understands their heritage.

About Us

HIMAYA, the Heritage Institute of Mission Art for Yielding of Appreciation Heritage, was founded in the heart of Palawan, Philippines, as a response to the urgent need to protect and celebrate the province's diverse cultural and artistic legacy. Established in 2013, this institute emerged from a shared vision among a group of passionate individuals, including cultural advocates, artists, historians, and community leaders, who recognized the significance of Palawan's unique heritage.

The name "HIMAYA" itself encapsulates the agency's mission, with "HIMAYA" signifying "glory" in the local dialect. The institute's founders believed that fostering glory was key to preserving Palawan's rich cultural tapestry. HIMAYA embodies the essence of embracing and preserving Palawan's heritage as its core mandate.

This very day, HIMAYA stands as a beacon of hope for Palawan's cultural and artistic legacy, working tirelessly to ensure that this heritage remains vibrant, celebrated, and cherished by generations to come. With a dedicated team and a strong network of collaborators, HIMAYA continues to write the story of Palawan's cultural preservation and appreciation for the world to embrace.

Objectives:

  • Heritage Conservation: We establish and maintain heritage conservation centers dedicated to the preservation and restoration of traditional artifacts, manuscripts, and artworks. These centers serve as hubs for research and conservation efforts.
  • Cultural Promotion and Education: The agency seeks to promote Palawan's cultural and artistic legacy through educational programs, exhibitions, and events. It aims to foster an understanding and appreciation of Palawan's rich heritage among both locals and visitors.
  • Cultural Exchange: The agency actively promotes cultural exchange programs, both nationally and internationally, to share Palawan's cultural diversity and learn from others. These exchanges foster mutual understanding and appreciation of different cultures.
  • Cultural Tourism: The agency develops sustainable cultural tourism initiatives that allow visitors to engage with Palawan's cultural heritage while contributing to the local economy. These initiatives help raise awareness and generate revenue for heritage preservation.
  • Policy Advocacy: HIMAYA advocates for policies that protect and promote Palawan's cultural heritage, including securing funding for cultural preservation projects and enforcing laws against cultural appropriation.

Discover Palawan

Palawan, a land locked in time. Whether it be the magnificent natural wonders, ancient artifacts and handicrafts, or indigenous beliefs and dialects, there’s a time-honored quality about all things Palawan, which has preserved its cultural glory for centuries. Palawan’s distinct charm lies in its harmonious blend of natural wonders and cultural richness. The warmth and hospitality of its local communities of Palaw’an, combined with its ecological significance, have earned it the well-deserved title of “The Last Ecological Frontier of the Philippines.” Though, these ideas merely scratch the surface of the true depth of Palawan’s culture and heritage.

Palawan's history is a tapestry of cultural influences and historical events that have shaped the island's unique identity. This gives credence to the many reasons it is known as the “last frontier of the Philippines.” The earliest known cultural influence can be attributed to pre-colonial Austronesian inhabitants of the province, the Palaw’ans and Tagbanuas people, who have yielded some of the best works of indigenous art. The most well-known being Burial Jars, also known as the “ship-of-the-dead,” the Manunggul Jar is a burial container that represents the journey of the soul to the afterlife. Another significant foundation of the culture of Palawan hails from the province’s most marginalized sector. For fifteen centuries, Palawan was under Muslim rule while influenced by the Sulu Sultanate of the southern Philippines. Despite the near eradication of this ancient Muslim culture, remnants of its influence still stand today, through the vibrant colors used by the Muslim mat weavers in Brooke's Point. Muslim weavers of Palawan create such intricate mats, and despite its common title of banig, stands out in the splendor of vivid colors and patterns woven into its very fibers. Perhaps the most threatened aspect of Palawan’s culture are the ancient beliefs and traditions of the indigenous tribes of the province. Vocal and musical traditions are one of the more well-known ways that these traditions are kept in ties with the modern age. Bagit is an important part of the Filipino Folk Culture in Puerto Princesa. Bird, snake, insect, leaf, and element sounds, as well as birds’, monkeys’, snakes’, and insect chirping, can all be heard in this type of instrumental music. It’s usually played on a wooden, three-stringed lute called a kusyapi.

Humanity’s never-ending advancement has threatened the many cultural and ecological markers that herald Palawan as “The Last Frontier.” Palawan is more than just the pristine islands and natural wonders of tourist fame; it is a melting pot of different cultures that have made incredible works of arts and traditions, that have been overlooked for far too long. These are the last ties that we have to the works of our predecessors. The glory of their legacy must be preserved, as such it is the duty of their successors to take up the torch that they have lit, to light the path of an unsung history.

Vision and Mission

Vision Statement:

At HIMAYA, we envision a Palawan where the vibrant tapestry of cultural traditions and artistic expressions flourishes, ensuring that the legacy of our ancestors continues to inspire and enrich our lives. We aspire to be the driving force behind a community that proudly embraces, safeguards, and celebrates Palawan's unique heritage. Through education, innovation, and unwavering dedication, we aim to weave the threads of tradition and creativity into a future that is deeply rooted in our past, where Palawan's rich cultural and artistic legacy serves as a beacon of identity, unity, and inspiration for all.

Mission Statement:

HIMAYA, the Heritage Institute of Mission Art for Yielding of Appreciation, was founded with the primary mission of safeguarding and promoting the rich cultural and artistic legacy of Palawan, Philippines. We strive to preserve the rich cultural heritage of this extraordinary province, celebrating its indigenous customs, languages, artistry, and historical legacies. Through collaborative efforts and community engagement, we aim to ensure that Palawan's traditions continue to flourish, connecting past and present generations while embracing the cultural diversity that makes Palawan a true treasure. Our dedication is driven by the belief that these traditions are not just a part of Palawan's identity but also a source of pride and inspiration for the future.
